Foundation

How to Use This Planner

The Morning Priority Principle

Every top-producing real estate agent follows one non-negotiable discipline: time-block every morning and complete the activities that drive your future business before anything else. Door closed. Email closed. Phone in do-not-disturb mode. Revenue-generating work comes first — always. This planner is built around that principle. Every day starts with a protected MORNING PRIORITY BLOCK from 9:00–11:00 AM dedicated exclusively to lead generation and prospecting. Guard it with your life. If you complete your most important work before noon, the rest of the day is a bonus.

1
Fill in your Quarterly Goals first

Before Week 1 begins, complete the Quarterly Goals page and Referral Partner Roster. Know your numbers — listings, closings, and GCI targets — before you start the clock.

2
Start each week with the Weekly Plan page

Every Monday morning begins here. Set your weekly targets, write your lead gen call list (FSBOs, expireds, SOI), and commit to your listing appointment goals before you dial.

3
Use each Daily Page like a contract

Pre-fill your call names the night before. Check off activities as you complete them. The daily page is your scoreboard — not a wish list. Treat it like a court-ordered obligation.

4
Log your KPIs every single day

The daily KPI bar at the bottom of each page takes 60 seconds to fill in. At the end of the quarter, these numbers will tell you exactly why your results are what they are.

5
End each day with a Win or Gratitude

Top producers maintain a positive mindset by acknowledging what went right. Write at least one win, referral received, or gratitude before closing the planner each evening.

6
Complete the Quarterly Review

At the end of 13 weeks, fill in the Quarterly Review before starting your next planner. Review, adjust, and commit to improvements. The cycle of growth continues.

Core Philosophy

8 Laws of the Top-Producing Real Estate Agent

Law 01
Prospect First. Always.

Revenue-generating activities own your morning. Admin and email are scheduled around prospecting — never the reverse. Your morning priority block is sacred. Protect it every day.

Law 02
Listings Are Your Leverage

Every listing is a billboard, a buyer lead magnet, and a dual-sided commission opportunity. The listing agent builds the brand. Listings create market presence that cold calls cannot buy.

Law 03
Consistency Beats Intensity

Five prospecting calls every single day outperforms 50 calls once a month. Daily habits compound into extraordinary results. Show up every day — especially when it's inconvenient.

Law 04
Accountability Changes Behavior

A coach, an accountability partner, or a daily check-in dramatically increases follow-through. Commit publicly to your numbers. This planner is your silent accountability partner.

Law 05
Delegate to Protect Your Time

Every hour spent on transaction coordination, paperwork, or scheduling is stolen from listing appointments, buyer consultations, and relationship-building. Build a team. Leverage everything.

Law 06
Measure Leading Indicators

Track dials made, listing appointments set, and referrals asked for — not just closings. Activity metrics predict future results. You can't manage what you don't measure.

Law 07
Trust Outlasts Every Transaction

Handwritten notes, pop-bys, anniversary calls, and the 33-touch system generate more referrals than any advertising. Be their agent for life — not just for one sale.

Law 08
Rest Is Part of the System

A hard stop at 5 PM and intentional weekends are non-negotiable. Burnout destroys long-term production. The most productive agents work fewer, more focused hours — not more hours.

Key Concepts Used in This Planner
Morning Priority Block (9–11 AM)
A protected, distraction-free window every morning dedicated exclusively to lead generation. No email, no showings, no exceptions. FSBOs, expireds, circle prospecting, and SOI calls happen here. This is your most important production time each day.
Listing Consultation (Listing Presentation)
A structured discovery meeting with a potential seller designed to understand their goals, timeline, and motivation before presenting your CMA and marketing plan. Leads with curiosity, not a pitch. Educates on the market before recommending a price. Builds trust and wins the listing agreement.
The 33-Touch System (Gary Keller)
Contact every person in your database 33 times per year through a strategic mix of calls, handwritten notes, pop-bys, emails, events, and mailers. At 200 people in your database, this produces approximately 4 closings per year from the database alone. Scale to 2,500 people to expect 50+ closings annually.
A / B / C Partner Tiers
A system for prioritizing referral partners. A-Tier: preferred lenders, title reps, estate attorneys — contact every 2–4 weeks. B-Tier: CPAs, inspectors, financial advisors — contact monthly. C-Tier: moving companies, HR directors, property managers — contact quarterly to build the relationship.
KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)
The daily and weekly numbers that predict your future results: dials made, listing appointments set and held, listings taken, contracts written, referrals asked for, and closings. Tracking these consistently shows you exactly what's driving — or limiting — your production.
Transaction Management
The practice of proactively communicating with all parties on every active transaction from contract to close. Status calls before they ask you — to all clients, co-op agents, lenders, and title. Eliminates reactive stress, prevents surprises, and builds a reputation for reliability that generates referrals.

Keep This Page Visible

Weekly Time-Block Reference

🔴 The Morning Priority Rule: 9:00–11:00 AM Every Day is PROTECTED. No showings. No email. No exceptions. Lead generate first — your future business depends on it.
Monday— Lead Generation Day (FSBOs / Expireds / Circle Prospecting)
  • FSBO calls: empathetic introduction — "I work with buyers who may be perfect for your home"; offer market data as value
  • Expired listing calls: "I know how frustrating it is when a home doesn't sell. Can I share what I'd do differently?"
  • Circle prospecting: call 50–100 homes around a new listing or sold property — "I have buyers looking in your neighborhood"
  • Referral partner lunch at 12–1 PM
  • Open house / market preview at 1–3 PM
  • CRM update, thank-you notes, weekly goal log at 4–5 PM
Tuesday— Listing Appointment Day
  • SOI calls 9–11 AM: genuine check-in with 10–15 sphere contacts — always end with "Who do you know thinking of buying or selling?"
  • Listing appointments 1–4 PM: CMA, marketing plan, trust before asking to sign; handle every objection confidently
  • New listing onboarding at end of day: photography, staging, MLS entry scheduled immediately
  • New referral source outreach: CPAs, attorneys, financial planners in the 9–11 AM block
  • Log KPIs: listing appts held, listing agreements signed
Wednesday— Buyers & Transaction Management Day
  • Past client database calls 9–11 AM: annual home equity review; "Want a free CMA? The market has moved."
  • Proactive transaction status calls: every client and co-op agent on every active deal — before they call you
  • Buyer consultations 1–3 PM: structured discovery first; know their goals before showing homes
  • Property showings for active buyer clients
  • Monthly market update email sent to full database
  • CRM clean-up, new contacts added, 33-touch sequences updated
Thursday— Database, Referrals & Sphere Day (Buffini Model)
  • Pop-bys 9–11 AM: bring a meaningful, personal item (coffee, seasonal gift) — this is Buffini's highest-converting touch
  • Handwritten notecards: write 5–10 personal notes to database contacts (not mass mailers — personal notes only)
  • Open house follow-up: call every visitor from the prior weekend within 48 hours while memory is fresh
  • Referral thank-you: send a personalized thank-you within 24 hours of every referral received
  • Networking lunch with preferred lender, title rep, or CPA at 12–1 PM
Friday— Marketing, Planning & Professional Development
  • Review request calls for all recent closings — Google and Zillow — personal asks, not automated emails
  • Batch-record 1–2 short videos; schedule 5 social posts for next week in one sitting
  • 2 hours professional development: coaching podcast (Tom Ferry, Buffini), training module, script practice, MREA or The ONE Thing
  • Block all 5 lead gen windows in next week's calendar — non-negotiable
  • Full weekly KPI review vs. targets — are you on pace for the quarter?
  • Celebrate the week's wins with your team. Then log off.
5:00 PM Hard Stop — Every day. Protect your life outside work. Burnout kills long-term production. Real Estate Agent Success Planner • Quarterly Edition
